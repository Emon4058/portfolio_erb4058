Overview

The Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of a 6‑Degree‑of‑Freedom (DOF) Robotic Arm was conducted to evaluate structural integrity, stress distribution, and deformation across critical poses. The arm can carry a 20 kg payload when retracted and 9 kg when fully extended. A self‑collision avoidance system is integrated at the kinematic level to prevent interference between links.

We performed the analysis in ANSYS Static Structural, using realistic boundary conditions and nonlinear constraints. Throughout development, we maintained a minimum Factor of Safety (FoS) of 5, iterating the design 8 times to optimize mass and stiffness.

Key focus areas: identifying stress concentrations at joints, validating link thicknesses, and ensuring acceptable tip deflection under worst‑case loading.

Loads & Boundary Conditions

Boundary conditions were applied at the base mounting plate (fixed support), with payload forces applied at the end‑effector along gravity and off‑axis directions to simulate dynamic pick‑and‑place scenarios. Joint interfaces were modeled with appropriate contact and constraint assumptions, including nonlinearities where necessary.

Assumptions: simplified fillets where appropriate, fastener preloads abstracted as constraints, and conservative material properties from datasheets.

  • Fixed support at base; additional fixtures for mounting verification
  • Payload: 20 kg (retracted), 9 kg (extended)
  • Self‑collision clearance constraints respected in solved poses

Meshing Strategy & Results

Mesh density was refined at fillets, bolt interfaces, and abrupt section transitions. A convergence study ensured that peak von Mises stresses stabilized within an acceptable tolerance.

  • Element type: tetrahedral, with local refinement at stress risers
  • Convergence: monitored peak stress and tip deflection
  • Validation: FoS ≥ 5 across critical load cases; tip deflection within tolerance

Result highlights: redistributed thickness, material changes for select brackets, and geometry smoothing to reduce stress concentration.

Design Iterations

  • Increased cross‑section at high‑stress links while reducing mass on low‑stress members
  • Material swap on brackets to stainless where fatigue margins were low
  • Fillet optimization and removal of sharp features to reduce stress concentrations
  • Reinforced mounting points and verified bolt patterns against shear and tension

Outcomes

The analysis resulted in a robust, weight‑optimized design with comfortable margins. Stress concentrations were addressed, tip deflection met performance targets, and the structure demonstrated consistent FoS ≥ 5 in validated scenarios.
